Mediass AI Visual Symptom Image-Text Seed

Purpose:
This file trains retrieval for text descriptions of visible symptoms. The current backend does not analyze raw images directly; it can use text that a user types or OCR/caption text extracted from an image.

Reference policy:
The notes below are original summaries. Cite the listed reference when using the matching section.

Visual sign: cyanosis or blue lips
Description words users may use:
- blue lips, bluish fingers, blue skin, grey lips, dusky color.
Clinical meaning:
- Can indicate low oxygen, poor circulation, severe lung/heart disease, cold exposure, or shock. Interpret with breathing difficulty, chest pain, confusion, or low oxygen readings.
Triage:
- Blue lips with shortness of breath, chest pain, confusion, fainting, or severe weakness is an emergency.
Reference:
- Merck Manual Professional Edition: "Dyspnea."
- URL: https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/pulmonary-disorders/symptoms-of-pulmonary-disorders/dyspnea

Visual sign: jaundice or yellow eyes
Description words users may use:
- yellow eyes, yellow skin, dark urine, pale stool, itching, right upper belly pain.
Clinical meaning:
- Can relate to liver, gallbladder, bile duct, blood breakdown, infection, medication, or alcohol-related causes.
Triage:
- Jaundice with fever, severe abdominal pain, confusion, vomiting, pregnancy, or bleeding needs urgent medical evaluation.
Reference:
- Merck Manual Professional/Consumer clinical references on jaundice and hepatobiliary symptoms.
- URL: https://www.merckmanuals.com/

Visual sign: swelling of face, lips, tongue, or throat
Description words users may use:
- swollen lips, puffy face, tongue swelling, throat closing, hives, allergy.
Clinical meaning:
- Can reflect allergic reaction or angioedema. Airway involvement is dangerous.
Triage:
- Tongue/throat swelling, trouble breathing, wheezing, fainting, or widespread hives after exposure is an emergency.
Reference:
- Merck Manual Professional/Consumer clinical references on allergic reactions and anaphylaxis.
- URL: https://www.merckmanuals.com/

Visual sign: leg swelling
Description words users may use:
- swollen ankles, one leg bigger, calf swelling, pitting edema, red painful calf.
Clinical meaning:
- Bilateral swelling can relate to fluid retention, heart/ kidney/liver issues, venous disease, medications. One-sided painful swelling can suggest clot or injury.
Triage:
- One-sided leg swelling with chest pain or shortness of breath is urgent/emergency because of possible clot migration.
Reference:
- Merck Manual Professional Edition: "Dyspnea" for dyspnea with leg swelling and cardiovascular/pulmonary causes.
- URL: https://www.merckmanuals.com/professional/pulmonary-disorders/symptoms-of-pulmonary-disorders/dyspnea

Visual sign: bruising, petechiae, or nonblanching purple rash
Description words users may use:
- tiny purple dots, bruises without injury, rash does not fade when pressed, purple spots, bleeding gums.
Clinical meaning:
- Can reflect trauma, medication effects, platelet/clotting problems, infection, or vasculitis.
Triage:
- Fever plus nonblanching rash, confusion, neck stiffness, severe illness, or unusual bleeding is urgent.
Reference:
- StatPearls / NCBI Bookshelf: dermatology and fever/infection evaluation references.
- URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/

Visual sign: red painful swollen skin
Description words users may use:
- hot red skin, spreading redness, swollen wound, pus, streaking, tender skin.
Clinical meaning:
- Can indicate skin infection, abscess, inflammation, or allergic reaction. Fever and rapid spread increase concern.
Triage:
- Rapidly spreading redness, severe pain, fever, immune compromise, diabetes, facial involvement, or red streaking needs urgent evaluation.
Reference:
- StatPearls / NCBI Bookshelf: skin infection and rash evaluation references.
- URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/

Visual sign: blistering or peeling rash
Description words users may use:
- blisters, skin peeling, mouth sores, eye redness, painful rash, new medicine rash.
Clinical meaning:
- Can be benign in some cases, but painful widespread blistering with mucosal involvement can indicate severe drug reaction or serious infection.
Triage:
- Blistering or peeling rash with fever, pain, mouth/eye/genital sores, or new medication exposure is urgent/emergency.
Reference:
- StatPearls / NCBI Bookshelf: "Cutaneous Adverse Drug Reaction."
- URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK533000/
